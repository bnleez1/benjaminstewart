# Stage 1 - Desired Results
## Established Goals
- **Communication:**
- Interpersonal Communication: I can communicate and exchange information on a variety of familiar topics using phrases and simple sentences.
- **Cultures:**
- Relating Cultural Practices to Perspectives: I can use the language to investigate, explain, and reflect on the relationship between the practices and perspectives of the cultures studied.
- **Connections:**
- Making Connections: I can use the language to investigate, explain, and reflect on the concept of social connection through the lens of the Spanish language.
### Transfer
Learners will be able to independently use their learning to navigate a social gathering (e.g., a party, a caf√©, a new school) where they can confidently introduce themselves, engage in small talk about their lives and families, and interact with service personnel to order food and drinks.
### Meaning
### Understandings:
Learners will understand that...
- Language is a bridge to building new relationships and understanding diverse communities.
- Our daily routines and social interactions are shaped by cultural norms and values.
- Effective communication involves both understanding words and interpreting social cues.
### Essential Questions:
- How do we use language to build connections with others?
- What can we learn about a culture through its social customs?
- How does what we say, and how we say it, change in different situations?
### Acquisition
### _Learners will know‚Ä¶_
- **Week 1 (Saludar):** Greetings, goodbyes, and expressions of courtesy; how to ask and provide names, origins, and basic personal information.
- **Week 2 (Rutinas diarias):** Vocabulary for daily activities (e.g., waking up, eating, studying); reflexive verbs and telling time.
- **Week 3 (Familiares y amigos):** Vocabulary for family members and friends; descriptive adjectives and the verb _ser_.
- **Week 4 (Ordenar comida):** Vocabulary for common food and drinks; phrases for ordering in a restaurant or caf√© and expressing preferences.
### _Students will be skilled at‚Ä¶_
- Asking and answering simple questions about personal details.
- Describing their daily routine.
- Describing their family and friends.
- Ordering food and drinks and expressing preferences.
# Stage 2 - Assessment Evidence

> [!important] All graded assignments are listed below.
## Performance Task:
- **"Fiesta de Amigos" Simulation:**
- Students will participate in a simulated party where they will be expected to:
1. Introduce themselves to at least two new people.
2. Ask and answer questions about their daily routines and families.
3. Order a snack and a drink from a "concession stand."
- This task will be assessed using a rubric that evaluates fluency, accuracy, and task completion.
## Other Evidence:
- **Quizzes:** Weekly quizzes on vocabulary and grammar.
- **Oral Presentations:** A short presentation on their daily routine (Week 2).
- **Written Descriptions:** A short paragraph describing a family member or friend (Week 3).
- **Role-Playing:** In-class role-playing scenarios for each weekly theme.
- **Exit Tickets:** Short, informal checks for understanding at the end of lessons.

## Learning Plan (WHERETO
### **Week 1: Saludar / Can introduce oneself and others**
- **W (Where/Why):** Introduce the unit's essential questions and the final "Fiesta de Amigos" performance task.
- **H (Hook):** Show a video montage of greetings in various Spanish-speaking countries to spark interest.
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Use call-and-response, songs, and pair work to practice greetings, introductions, and asking for/giving personal information.
- **R (Rethink/Reflect):** Students will reflect on the differences between formal and informal greetings.
- **E (Evaluate):** Observe pair work and give a short quiz on greetings and introductions.
- **T (Tailor):** Provide sentence starters for students who need more support and encourage more advanced students to ask follow-up questions.
- **O (Organize):** Begin with basic greetings, then move to asking and answering questions, and finally, short conversations.
### **Week 2: Rutinas diarias / Can describe daily routines and activities**
- **W:** Connect daily routines to getting to know someone on a deeper level.
- **H:** Start with a "A Day in My Life" picture story from a teenager in a Spanish-speaking country.
- **E:** Use gestures, charades, and storytelling to introduce reflexive verbs and vocabulary for daily routines.
- **R:** Students will create a visual representation of their daily routine and compare it with a partner's.
- **E:** Students will give a short oral presentation about their daily routine.
- **T:** Provide a template for the presentation to guide students.
- **O:** Focus on vocabulary acquisition first, then verb conjugation, and finally, the presentation.
### **Week 3: Familiares y amigos / Can describe family and friends**
- **W:** Emphasize the importance of family and friends in Spanish-speaking cultures.
- **H:** Students bring in a photo of a family member or friend and share one thing about them in English to build a personal connection to the topic.
- **E:** Use family trees, descriptive adjectives, and the verb _ser_ to equip students to describe people.
- **R:** Students will write a short paragraph describing the person in their photo.
- **E:** Assess the written description and give a quiz on family vocabulary and adjectives.
- **T:** Allow students to choose the person they want to describe to increase engagement.
- **O:** Build from vocabulary to descriptive phrases, and then to a cohesive paragraph.
### **Week 4: Ordenar comida / Can order food and drink**
- **W:** Frame this week as the final preparation for the "Fiesta de Amigos."
- **H:** Create a "caf√©" atmosphere in the classroom with menus and tablecloths.
- **E:** Use menus, role-playing, and videos to teach food vocabulary and ordering phrases.
- **R:** Students will practice ordering in pairs, switching between the roles of customer and server.
- **E:** The "Fiesta de Amigos" performance task will serve as the summative assessment for this week and the unit.
- **T:** Use menus with pictures to support all learners.
- **O:** The week will be dedicated to practicing and preparing for the final performance task.
