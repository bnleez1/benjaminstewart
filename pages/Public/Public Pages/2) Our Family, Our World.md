### **Module 2: Purpose & Desired Results**
**Essential Question:** How do different family structures shape who we are and how we relate to others?
**Purpose:** The purpose of this module is to apply your reading strategies to understand texts about families and relationships. Family is a universal topic, and being able to read and talk about it is essential for making deeper personal connections. We will practice extracting different layers of information from a text and build our vocabulary for describing the people closest to us.
**Desired Results:** By the end of this five-day module, you will be able to:
- Obtain general information to understand the overall structure and dynamic of a family described in a text.
- Scan a text to find specific information about family members, such as their roles and relationships.
- Identify the meaning of new vocabulary related to family by using clues from the surrounding context.
- Use key grammar and vocabulary, including possessives and descriptive adjectives, to talk about family structures.

### **Reading: A Portrait of Modern Families**
What does the word "family" mean to you? For many people, it means a mother, a father, and children. For others, it includes grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins living together. And for some, family can be one parent and a child, or a couple with beloved pets. Families around the world are diverse and beautiful in many ways, and reading about them requires an openness to different cultural and social contexts.
In this lesson, we will read about a few different families. As active participants in your learning, you will practice two important reading skills. First, we will practice getting **general information**, which is like looking at a photograph from a distance to see the whole picture. Then, we will find **specific information**, like using a magnifying glass to see the small, important details. Let's begin.
**The Rossi Family: A Nuclear Family in Florence**
The Rossi family lives in a small apartment in Florence, Italy. They are a **nuclear family**, which typically means two parents and their children. Paolo, the father, is a chef at a local restaurant. His wife, Sofia, is a part-time language tutor. They have two children. Their son, Leo, is ten years old, and their daughter, Mia, is seven. Paolo works long hours, so he is often tired, but he always makes time for his family on Sundays. On that day, they usually go to the park or visit the children's grandparents, who live in another city. Sofia is very organized and manages the busy **household**. She helps the children with their homework and makes sure everyone is happy and healthy. The children have a close relationship. Leo is protective of his younger sister, and Mia looks up to her big brother.

**Mr. Chen and Mei: A Single-Parent Family**
In a quiet neighborhood in Singapore lives Mr. Chen and his twelve-year-old daughter, Mei. Mr. Chen is a single parent. His wife died when Mei was very young, so he has raised her on his own. He works as a bus driver, which means he sometimes has to work very early in the morning or late at night. Because of this, Mei is a very independent and **responsible** girl. She helps with chores like washing the dishes and keeping her room tidy. They don't have a big family, but their bond is incredibly strong. Every Friday night is their special "movie night," where they order pizza and watch a film together. Mr. Chen's main goal is to provide a good education and a happy life for his daughter.
Sometimes, when you read, you must identify the meaning of vocabulary through context. Look at the word **responsible**. The text says Mei helps with chores because her father works long hours. From this context, you can infer that "responsible" means she is reliable and does the things she needs to do.
