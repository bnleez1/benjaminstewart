    ### **1. Introduction (10%)**
    
    - **Hook (5%)**: Engages the reader‚Äôs interest from the beginning.
    - **Context of the problem (5%)**: Provides necessary background information to understand the thesis.
    - **Thesis Statement (10%)**: Clearly presents the main argument or point of the essay.
    
    ### **2. Body Paragraphs (20%)**
    
    - **Topic Sentences (15%)**: Each paragraph begins with a clear topic sentence that relates to the thesis.
    - **Evidence and Examples (15%)**: Provides relevant and sufficient evidence to support the topic sentences.
    - **Analysis (15%)**: Explains how the evidence supports the thesis and topic sentences.
    - **Link or summary (5%)**: Segues from the main idea from the current body paragraph to the main idea of the next body paragraph or summarizes or concludes current body paragraph.
    
    ### **3. Conclusion (10%)**
    
    - **Restatement of Thesis (10%)**: Reiterates the main argument in a new way.
    - **States significance of the thesis (5%)**: Briefly states the significance, relevance to different contexts, projects future considerations, and/or provides big ideas that were discussed in the essay.
    - **Closing Statement (5%)**: Ends with a final thought or call to action that leaves a lasting impression.
    
    ### **4. Organization and Coherence (10%)**
    
    - **Logical Flow (5%)**: Ideas are presented in a logical order, making the essay easy to follow.
    - **Transitions (5%)**: Smooth transitions between paragraphs and ideas within paragraphs.
    
    ### **5. Grammar, Mechanics, and Style (50%)**
    
    - **Grammar and Syntax (5%)**: Correct use of grammar and sentence structure.
    - **Punctuation and Spelling (3%)**: Accurate punctuation and spelling throughout the essay.
    - **Style and Tone (2%)**: Consistent and appropriate style and tone for an academic essay.
    
    ### Total: 100%
