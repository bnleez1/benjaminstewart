- At the bottom of the story, create a section for each group member to answer the following questions (in complete sentences) and vocabulary focus (matching exercise).
    
    ## **Part A ‚Äì The Arrival**
    
    **Comprehension Questions**
    
    1. How does Maria feel as the boat approaches the island?
    2. What do the cliffs look like?
    3. What unusual shapes does Maria think she sees?
    4. How is the air described near the island?
    
    **Vocabulary Focus**
    
    - Match the words with their meanings:
        
        1. **Jagged**
        2. **Silhouette**
        3. **Metallic tang**
        4. **Cove**
        
        a. A small, sheltered bay
        
        b. An outline of a shape, usually dark against light
        
        c. Having sharp, rough edges
        
        d. A taste or smell like metal
        
    
    ---
    
    ## **Part B ‚Äì The Forest Path**
    
    **Comprehension Questions**
    
    1. What makes the forest canopy unusual?
    2. What is strange about the vines?
    3. Which sounds do the explorers hear in the forest?
    4. How are the flowers described?
    
    **Vocabulary Focus**
    
    - Fill in the blanks with the correct word:
        1. The forest air was heavy and **_____** (thick / thin).
        2. The vines seemed to **_____** around the trees (curl / snap).
        3. The flowers had bright and **_____** colors (dull / vivid).
        4. A butterfly with **_____** wings passed by (glass-like / broken).
    
    ---
    
    ## **Part C ‚Äì The Abandoned Village**
    
    **Comprehension Questions**
    
    1. How are the huts described?
    2. What do the carvings on the well look like?
    3. What strange object hangs from a tree?
    4. What makes the group uneasy in the village?
    
    **Vocabulary Focus**
    
    - Decide if the statements are **True (T)** or **False (F):**
        1. The huts looked new and well-kept. ___
        2. The carvings on the well included spirals and stars. ___
        3. The swing was moving even though there was no wind. ___
        4. The group saw many villagers walking around. ___
    
    ---
    
    ## **Part D ‚Äì The Tower on the Hill**
    
    **Comprehension Questions**
    
    1. How does the tower look from a distance?
    2. What covers the outside of the tower?
    3. What strange carvings are seen at the entrance?
    4. How does the wind make the tower seem alive?
    
    **Vocabulary Focus**
    
    - Match the symbol with its description:
        
        1. Circles within circles
        2. Arrows pointing skyward
        3. An eye staring outward
        
        a. Suggests something is watching
        
        b. Represents movement or direction
        
        c. Symbol of endlessness or repetition
