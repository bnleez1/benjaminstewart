# **Unit 1: Who Am I? Who Are We?**
# Stage 1 - Desired Results
## Established Goals
This four-week unit introduces students to the fundamentals of personal information in English. Students will learn to identify main and specific ideas in texts, acquire vocabulary through context, and apply these skills to understand and share information about themselves, their families, friends, and roommates.
### Transfer
Students will be able to independently use their learning to 1) introduce themselves and others in various social contexts, 2) complete forms and documents that require personal information, 3) understand and engage in basic conversations about personal identity and relationships.
### Meaning
### Understandings:
Students will understand that:
- Sharing personal information is a key part of forming connections.
- The way we describe ourselves and our relationships reveals what we value.
- Effective communication involves both understanding the main points and the important details.
- We can often understand new words by looking at the words and sentences around them.
### Essential Questions:
- How do we define ourselves?
- What makes a family?
- What does it mean to be a good friend or roommate?
- How can we understand others better?
### Acquisition
### _Learners will know‚Ä¶_
Students will know:
- Vocabulary related to personal information (name, age, nationality, etc.).
- Vocabulary for family members, friends, and roommates.
- Basic sentence structures for asking and answering questions about personal information.
### _Students will be skilled at‚Ä¶_
Students will be skilled at:
- Identifying the main idea in a short text.
- Identifying specific details in a short text.
- Using context clues to determine the meaning of unfamiliar words.
- Obtaining general and specific information from a text.
# Stage 2 - Assessment Evidence

> [!important] All graded assignments are listed below.
## Performance Task:
**"All About Us" Class Directory**
Students will create a page for a class directory. Each student's page will include:
1. **A Personal Profile:** A short paragraph introducing themselves, including their name, age, where they are from, and a few of their interests.
2. **A Family or Friends Feature:** A short description of a family member, a close friend, or a roommate, including who they are and what they are like.
Students will present their page to a small group, and all pages will be compiled into a digital or physical class directory. This task will assess their ability to use vocabulary, identify and present key information, and communicate clearly.
## Other Evidence:
- Quizzes on vocabulary and key concepts.
- In-class activities and worksheets on identifying main ideas and specific details.
- "Exit tickets" at the end of a lesson to check for understanding.
- Informal observation of student participation in pair and group work.

## Learning Plan (WHERETO)
### **Week 1: Personal Information**
- **W (Where/Why):** Introduce the unit and the "All About Us" final project. Explain that learning to talk about ourselves is the first step to making new friends and connections.
- **H (Hook):** Start with a "get to know you" icebreaker activity.
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Introduce vocabulary for personal information. Students practice asking and answering questions in pairs. They will work with short texts to identify the main idea and specific details.
- **R (Rethink/Revise):** Students will review each other's practice sentences and offer feedback.
- **E (Evaluate):** Students will complete a short quiz on personal information vocabulary.
- **T (Tailor):** Provide sentence starters for students who need more support and encourage more advanced students to add more details.
- **O (Organize):** Lessons will be organized around introducing new vocabulary, practicing with partners, and then applying the skills to reading and writing tasks.
### **Week 2: Family**
- **W:** Connect the week's topic to the final project by explaining that they will now learn to talk about their families.
- **H:** Show pictures of different types of families (including pets as family!) and have students describe what they see.
- **E:** Introduce vocabulary for family members. Students will read short descriptions of families and identify general and specific information. They will practice using context to understand new words.
- **R:** In groups, students will create a family tree and explain it to their partners.
- **E:** Students will write a short paragraph about their own family or a famous family.
- **T:** Offer different graphic organizers for brainstorming ideas about their families.
- **O:** The week will build from vocabulary acquisition to reading comprehension and then to a short writing task.
### **Week 3: Friends and Roommates**
- **W:** Explain that we also have important relationships with friends and roommates and that this week will help them describe these people for their final project.
- **H:** Start with a class discussion: "What makes a good friend?"
- **E:** Introduce vocabulary for describing people's personalities. Students will read texts about friendships and roommate situations, focusing on identifying main ideas, specific details, and the meaning of words from context.
- **R:** Students will role-play conversations between friends or roommates.
- **E:** Students will complete a worksheet where they match vocabulary words to their definitions based on contextual clues.
- **T:** For the role-play, provide scripts for some students and allow others to create their own conversations.
- **O:** The week will be structured around reading, discussion, and interactive practice.
### **Week 4: Putting It All Together**
- **W:** Remind students of the "All About Us" project and show them a sample page.
- **H:** Have a "gallery walk" of the work they have done so far in the unit.
- **E:** Students will work on drafting and revising their pages for the class directory. Peer review sessions will be held.
- **R:** Students will practice presenting their pages to a partner before sharing with their small group.
- **E:** The final project (class directory page and presentation) will be the main evaluation for the unit.
- **T:** Provide checklists and templates to help students organize their project pages.
- **O:** The week will be dedicated to applying all the skills learned in the unit to the final performance task.
