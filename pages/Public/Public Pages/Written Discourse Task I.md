## Original Text
[[Text Analysis]]
The police arrived, of course unexpectedly. The guests remained unaffected until drawing from the keg suddenly ceased. This wasn’t Tom’s first rodeo, as neighbors would come to expect something similar each Halloween. Trash in the front yard, loud music pumping in the back, it was a wonder that a man at his age would still feel compelled to carry out such shindigs. Yet, here they were. Tom knew the score. Friends of the family, the officers were only doing their jobs, trying to send the staggering home while remaining civil to Tom. Law enforcement could expect a nasty call about the ordeal from the Mayor herself should anything get ugly. Tom grew up with a silver spoon in his mouth, the entire community knew this. The neighbors dealt with it, his teachers complained back in the day, the cops often turned the other cheek, and his extended family would often go to great lengths to avoid him altogether. This was Tom’s life, a slew of regrettable events, overindulging in wine, women, and song. Had he known where this would lead, he might have made different choices as a young lad.
