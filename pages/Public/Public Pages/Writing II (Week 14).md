**Main Claim:**
"The implementation of universal basic income (UBI) has the potential to reduce poverty and inequality, as evidenced by pilot studies in various countries showing increased financial stability and well-being among participants (Forget, 2021; Hoynes & Rothstein, 2019)."
**Counterclaim:**
"Critics argue that UBI may disincentivize work and lead to increased government spending, potentially burdening taxpayers and creating economic instability (Martinelli, 2017)."
**Rebuttal:**
"However, evidence from UBI experiments suggests minimal negative effects on work participation (Tcherneva, 2020), and the long-term economic benefits of reduced poverty and increased consumer spending may outweigh the initial costs (Standing, 2017)."
**In this example:**
- The main claim advocates for UBI as a solution to poverty and inequality, citing evidence from pilot studies.
- The counterclaim raises concerns about potential negative economic impacts, supported by research.
- The rebuttal addresses these concerns with evidence suggesting minimal work disincentives and potential long-term economic benefits.
