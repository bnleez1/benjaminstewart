# Criteria
- Include an 8-12-word title for your essay: in bold, main words are capitalized, centered to the page.
- Use one of the [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Acceptable fonts (APA, 7th edition)]] and be consistent throughout your text.
- Use the title page provided and then begin your essay on the next page.
- The first page of your essay and your references page should each have a page break. You need at least three references for completing your make up essay.
- Double space your five-paragraph essay with no extra spacing between paragraphs and no extra space between the title of your essay (heading) and the first paragraph.
- Include a 0.5 inch or 1.25 centimeter indentation for each paragraph.
- Begin with an [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Introductory Paragraph]] and conclude with a [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Conclusion Paragraph]]. Each of the three body paragraphs should follow the [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/The M.E.A.L. Plan- A Comprehensive Approach to Paragraph Development]].
- Each of the five paragraphs should have five-to-eight sentences between 150-180 words.
- Except for the conclusion paragraph, each paragraph should have at least one citation.
- Each citation should have a reference and each reference should have a citation. If not, you have plagiarized. See [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Plagiarism- The copycat crime in academia and beyond]] and [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/UAA language departmental plagiarism policy 2024]].
- All citations should be parenthetical and should not mention the study, researchers, authors, etc. Just mention the results or specific details they provide. See [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Parenthetical Vs. Narrative Citations]].
- All citations should be facts, examples, statistics, or details that relate to the topic sentence (i.e,. the main idea of the body paragraph).
- Any citations not included within the sentence it refers to is an example of plagiarism.
- Any cases of plagiarism will result in a zero grade for the assignment.
- Review your notes from the semester to see which words to avoid: _very; important; it is important_, etc.; absolutes like _always, never_, etc.; [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Demonstratives]]; and linking verbs in the main clause of each topic sentence
- Topic sentences: If you are writing an [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Expository essay]] or a [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Winning hearts and minds- Persuasive vs. argumentative essays]], each topic sentence should support (in order) each of the three key points listed at the end of the thesis statement. If writing an [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/Argumentative Essay]], each topic sentence should support one of the following (in this order): initial claim, counterclaim, and rebuttal.
- Write your essay in the third person.
- Single space within each reference and double space between each reference.
- Use a French indentation for all references.
- Remove any extra spacing between references and between the references heading and first reference.
- Include a page break for the references page.
- Include citations and references according to APA (See [[../../../60 Public/50 Notes/Permanent Notes/APA Guide]].).
- Remove any extraneous information: notes to yourself, highlighted text, comments, images, etc.
