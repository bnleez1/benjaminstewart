> [!important] This page discusses appropriate academic writing style, focusing on two main areas: the overuse of boosting (expressing excessive confidence in claims) and the nuances of hedging (expressing appropriate caution). It also covers avoiding figurative language, using active voice over passive, eliminating sexist/biased language, and reducing expressions like "there is/are" to create more formal, precise, and inclusive academic writing.
  

> [!important] This page covers various aspects of effective academic writing, including emphasis techniques, parallelism, sentence variety, completeness, conciseness, and appropriate language use - all aimed at creating clear, precise, and persuasive formal writing that avoids common errors such as sentence fragments and wordiness.

> [!important] This page explores the concepts of boosting and hedging in academic writing, where boosting involves using intensifiers to express confidence in claims (which should generally be avoided in academic contexts), while hedging employs cautious language to acknowledge uncertainty and limitations. Both techniques require careful balance: excessive boosting can lead to overgeneralizations, while appropriate hedging adds nuance and credibility to academic writing.

> [!important] The Paramedic Method is a seven-step editing technique developed by Richard Lanham that helps writers create clearer, more concise, and user-centered professional writing by eliminating unnecessary words, activating sentences, and removing passive voice. This systematic approach reduces word count while making prose more persuasive and readable through specific actions like circling prepositions, identifying linking verbs, and eliminating redundancies.
