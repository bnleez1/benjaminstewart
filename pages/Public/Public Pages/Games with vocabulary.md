Games are a great way to engage English Language Learners (ELLs) and help them practice vocabulary in a fun and interactive way. Here are 50 games that can be employed in the English language classroom:
1. **Bingo**: Create bingo cards with vocabulary words. Call out the definitions and have students mark the words on their cards.
2. **Charades**: Students act out a vocabulary word without speaking, while others guess the word.
3. **Pictionary**: Similar to Charades, but students draw the word instead of acting it out.
4. **Memory**: Create cards with vocabulary words and their definitions. Place them face down and have students find the matching pairs.
5. **20 Questions**: Think of a vocabulary word. Students ask up to 20 yes-or-no questions to guess the word.
6. **Word Search**: Create a word search puzzle with the vocabulary words.
7. **Crossword Puzzle**: Create a crossword puzzle using vocabulary words and their definitions as clues.
8. **Hangman**: Play the classic game of Hangman using vocabulary words.
9. **Scrabble**: Use the game Scrabble, but limit the words to the vocabulary list.
10. **Concentration**: Place vocabulary word cards face down. Students flip over two cards at a time, trying to find matching pairs.
11. **Jeopardy**: Create a Jeopardy-style game with categories related to the vocabulary.
12. **Taboo**: Describe a vocabulary word without using certain “taboo” words.
13. **Hot Potato**: Pass an object around while music plays. When the music stops, the student holding the object must define a vocabulary word.
14. **Battleship**: Use a Battleship grid with vocabulary words. Students guess coordinates to find their opponent’s words.
15. **Balderdash**: Students invent definitions for obscure vocabulary words, and others guess which definition is correct.
16. **Word Ladder**: Change one letter of a word to create a new word, using as many vocabulary words as possible.
17. **Categories**: Call out a category related to the vocabulary, and students list as many words as they can that fit the category.
18. **Who Am I?**: Attach a vocabulary word to each student’s back. They ask yes-or-no questions to figure out their word.
19. **Word Association**: Say a word, and students say the first word that comes to mind. Try to link it back to the vocabulary.
20. **Flashcard Relay**: Students race to match flashcards of words and definitions.
21. **Sentence Scramble**: Scramble sentences using the vocabulary words and have students put them in the correct order.
22. **Word Wheel**: Spin a wheel with vocabulary words, and students must define the word it lands on.
23. **Fishing for Words**: Attach paperclips to flashcards and use a magnet on a string to “fish” for words. Define the word to keep your catch.
24. **Dictionary Race**: Students race to find the definition of a vocabulary word in the dictionary.
25. **Alphabet Soup**: Create a grid of letters with hidden vocabulary words. Students circle the words.
26. **Spelling Bee**: Conduct a spelling bee with the vocabulary words.
27. **Word Bingo**: Create bingo cards with definitions. Call out the words, and students mark the corresponding definition.
28. **Word Jumble**: Jumble the letters of vocabulary words and have students unscramble them.
29. **Fill in the Blanks**: Provide sentences with blanks for students to fill in with vocabulary words.
30. **Story Cubes**: Roll dice with pictures or words and create a story using the vocabulary words.
31. **Synonym Match**: Match vocabulary words with their synonyms.
32. **Antonym Match**: Match vocabulary words with their antonyms.
33. **Word Snake**: Create a snake of words where the last letter of one word is the first letter of the next.
34. **Role-Play**: Use the vocabulary words in role-play scenarios.
35. **Word Collage**: Create a collage of images and words related to the vocabulary.
36. **Guess the Word**: Give clues about a vocabulary word until students can guess it.
37. **Word Races**: Students race to the board to write as many related words as they can in a set time.
38. **Quiz Show**: Host a quiz show where students answer questions related to the vocabulary.
39. **Word Sort**: Sort words into categories based on their meaning or other criteria.
40. **Word Chain**: Form a circle. The first student says a word, and the next student must say a word that starts with the last letter of the previous word.
41. **Find Someone Who**: Students find classmates who can define the vocabulary words.
42. **Synonym and Antonym Bingo**: Create bingo cards with synonyms or antonyms of the vocabulary words.
43. **Vocabulary Trading Cards**: Students create trading cards for each vocabulary word with a definition, picture, and example sentence.
44. **Pyramid**: Create a pyramid of vocabulary words. Each row should have a word that relates to the words above and below it.
45. **Password**: Give one student the “password” (a vocabulary word). Other students ask yes-or-no questions to guess the password.
46. **Word of the Day**: Introduce a new vocabulary word each day and incorporate it into classroom activities.
47. **Prefix and Suffix Game**: Identify the prefix or suffix in vocabulary words and discuss how it affects the meaning.
48. **Vocabulary Skits**: Students create and perform short skits using the vocabulary words.
49. **Word Jigsaw**: Create jigsaw puzzle pieces with parts of vocabulary definitions. Students match the pieces to complete the definitions.
50. **Vocabulary Circles**: Each student says a vocabulary word and a sentence using the word. The next student repeats and adds a new word.
These games can make learning vocabulary more engaging and interactive, helping ELLs to better understand and retain new words.
