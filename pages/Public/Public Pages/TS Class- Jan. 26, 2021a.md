# Essential Understanding
Learners will understand that the objective of [[Thesis Seminar (archive)]] is to create a capstone research project related to key aspects of applied linguistics associated with a relevant and meaningful researchable problem, one that of interest to the learner based on past experiences either as a student and/or teacher.
# Essential Question
How can a [[Problem Statement]] reflect 1) the general [researchable topic](https://www.umflint.edu/library/how-select-research-topic), 2) an overall indirect question that represents a sound thesis statement, and 3) the significance or purpose of a pursuable research project?

    > [!important] As we begin the semester, it's important that you have an opportunity to express your expectations you have of me, your instructor, and of the course itself. Knowing your expectations from the beginning of class helps me better prepare my instruction according to your needs, wants, and personal preferences.     ## What are your expectations?
    
    1. What do you expect to get out of this course?
        1. What knowledge to you expect to gain?
        2. What skill sets do you expect to gain?
        3. What emotional support do you expect to receive?
    2. How might your prior experience either positively or negatively impact your learning outcomes in this course?
        1. Positive
        2. Negative
    3. As your instructor, what expectations do you have of me? How might I best assist you in accomplishing your learning goals?
        1. How I teach?
        2. How I assess?
        3. How I conduct myself?
        4. How accessible I am?
        5. etc.
    4. What specific [SMART goals](https://www.thoughtco.com/how-do-i-write-smart-goals-31493) do you have for this course? SMART goals are **S**pecific (based on a skill, knowledge, and/or attitudinal), **M**easurable (observable), **A**chievable (doable), **R**esults-oriented (outcome-related), and **T**ime-phased (within a semester)
