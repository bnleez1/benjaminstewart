# Stage 1 - Desired Results
## Established Goals
- Students will develop and practice their English language skills to improve their communication with others by being active participants within authentic cultural contexts and situations.
- **21st Century Skills**
    - Communication & Collaboration
    - Creativity & Innovation
    - Critical Thinking & Problem Solving
    - Cultural Awareness
### Transfer
Learners will be able to independently use their learning toâ€¦
- Articulate and defend a persuasive argument on social, cultural, or professional topics in both written (essay) and spoken (podcast) formats.
- Analyze and discuss authentic English-language media (articles, documentaries, podcasts) related to a variety of real-world subjects.
- Adapt their language and communication style to suit different contexts, such as academic discussions, professional interviews, and informal conversations about personal values and traditions.
- Navigate and describe complex personal and professional situations, such as job applications, cultural traditions, and interpersonal relationships.
### Meaning
### Understandings:
Learners will understand that...
- Effective communication requires adapting language and structure for a specific purpose (e.g., to inform, persuade, entertain) and audience.
- A persuasive argument is built on a clear claim supported by logical reasoning and relevant evidence.
- Cultural identity is expressed and understood through traditions, arts, consumer choices, and social values.
- Language is the primary vehicle for exploring and expressing personal and professional aspirations, values, and relationships.
### Essential Questions:
Learners will keep considering...
- How do we adapt our communication to effectively persuade others?
- What makes an argument or opinion compelling and convincing?
- How do our cultural traditions, consumer habits, and personal tastes shape who we are?
- How can we use language to articulate our future goals and build meaningful relationships?
### Acquisition
### _Learners will knowâ€¦_
- **Vocabulary related to:** Education, nature/environment, shopping/fashion, music, festivals/traditions, jobs/careers, holidays/celebrations, and relationships.
- **Grammar Concepts:**
    - Reported Speech
    - Passive Voice
    - Comparatives & Superlatives
    - Past Perfect Tense
    - Future Perfect Tense
    - First & Second Conditionals
    - Prepositions of Time
    - Relative Clauses
- **Discourse Features:** Structure of a five-paragraph persuasive essay (introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion), structure of a podcast/interview, conventions for writing a CV.
### _Students will be skilled atâ€¦_
- **Reading:** Analyzing articles and reviews on unit themes for main ideas, supporting details, and author's purpose.
- **Writing:** Composing a well-structured persuasive essay, writing a CV, and writing descriptive and narrative pieces (e.g., about a festival, a fashion trend).
- **Listening:** Comprehending details from authentic materials like podcasts, documentaries, and interviews.
- **Speaking:** Discussing complex topics, expressing and justifying opinions, participating in a mock interview, and co-creating a podcast.
  
# Stage 2 - Assessment Evidence

> [!important] All graded assignments are listed below.
## Performance Tasks:
1. **Podcast (35% - Part A):** In pairs or small groups, students will produce an 8-10 minute podcast on a topic that synthesizes at least two themes from Unit II (e.g., "The Environmental Impact of Fast Fashion," "How Music Shapes Holiday Traditions," "Education for Future Careers").
    - **Evaluation Criteria:** Creativity, fluency, pronunciation, cohesion, appropriate use of grammar and vocabulary.
2. **Persuasive Essay (35% - Part B):** Individually, students will write a five-paragraph persuasive essay on a debatable topic from Unit II.
    - **Evaluation Criteria:** Clear thesis, logical organization, strength of supporting details, coherence, use of English, and proper APA formatting.
## Other Evidence:
**Portfolio of Classwork (30%)**
- **Weekly Mini-Tasks:**
    - School brochure (Education)
    - Eco-project plan (Environment)
    - Fashion show presentation (Fashion)
    - Music playlist project (Music)
    - Festival presentation (Traditions)
    - CV and mock interview performance (Careers)
    - Holiday scrapbook page (Holidays)
    - Written relationship advice (Relationships)
- **Quizzes:** Short quizzes on key grammar points and vocabulary sets for each theme.
- **Observations:** Teacher observation of student participation in pair and group discussions.
- **Self/Peer-Assessment:** Students will use rubrics to provide feedback on drafts of the persuasive essay and practice runs of the podcast.
# Stage 3 - Learning Plan
## Learning Plan (WHERETO)
### **Week 1 (Course Weeks 9-10): Education & The Environment**
- **W (Where/Why):** The unit will be introduced by showing two contrasting clips: one of an innovative school and one of a pressing environmental issue. We will ask: "What does an ideal future look like, and how do we use our voice to advocate for it?" The final project (podcast/essay) will be introduced to show where we are heading.
- **H (Hook/Hold):** Hook students with a debate: "Should school uniforms be mandatory?" and hold interest by having them research an environmental issue they are passionate about.
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Students will read articles on different education systems and environmental problems. They will listen to educational podcasts and nature documentaries. We will explicitly teach and practice **reported speech** (for quoting sources) and the **passive voice** (common in formal/scientific writing).
- **R (Rethink/Reflect):** Students will reflect in a journal: "What was my most meaningful learning experience in school and why?" and "What is one change I can make in my daily life to help the environment?"
- **E (Evaluate):** Students will be evaluated on their participation in discussions and the completion of their **School Brochure** and **Eco-project Plan**.
- **T (Tailor):** Instruction will be tailored by allowing students to choose which educational models and environmental issues they research based on interest.
- **O (Organize):** The week will begin with vocabulary and grammar input, move to receptive skills practice (reading/listening), and culminate in productive, task-based outputs (discussions and mini-projects).
### **Week 2 (Course Weeks 11-12): Fashion & Music**
- **W (Where/Why):** We will connect these topics to the larger theme of identity and expression. We'll ask: "How do the clothes we wear and the music we listen to tell a story about who we are?"
- **H (Hook/Hold):** Hook students by having them bring in and discuss an object of clothing or a song that is meaningful to them. Hold interest by having them plan a "thematic fashion show" or a "soundtrack for their life."
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Students will read fashion blogs and music reviews. They will watch fashion and music interviews. We will teach **comparatives/superlatives** for making judgments and the **past perfect** for sequencing past events (e.g., "By the time I discovered the band, they had already released three albums").
- **R (Rethink/Reflect):** Students will discuss in groups: "Is fast fashion worth the ethical and environmental cost?" and "How has technology changed the way we experience music?"
- **E (Evaluate):** Evaluation will be based on the **Fashion Show Presentation** and the **Music Playlist Project**, which will require them to justify their choices using the target language.
- **T (Tailor):** Students will have complete freedom to choose the fashion styles and music genres they want to focus on for their projects.
- **O (Organize):** The week will be organized around personal expression, moving from individual reflection to collaborative project creation.
### **Week 3 (Course Weeks 13-14): Traditions & Careers**
- **W (Where/Why):** We will explore how we honor our past and plan for our future. We'll ask: "How do our traditions connect us to our community, and how do we prepare for our professional futures?"
- **H (Hook/Hold):** Hook students with a vibrant video montage of different cultural festivals around the world. Hold their interest by having them take a career aptitude quiz and discuss the results.
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Students will read articles about festivals and career advice. They will watch festival documentaries and sample job interviews. We will teach the **future perfect** ("By 2030, I will have finished my degree") and **first/second conditionals** for discussing future plans and hypothetical situations. Students will learn the format of a CV.
- **R (Rethink/Reflect):** Students will reflect on a family or community tradition that is important to them. They will also outline their five-year career plan and identify potential obstacles.
- **E (Evaluate):** Students will be evaluated on their **Festival Presentation**, their completed **CV**, and their performance in a **Mock Interview**.
- **T (Tailor):** Students can choose to present on a festival from their own culture or one that interests them. They can tailor their CV and mock interview to a job they are genuinely interested in.
- **O (Organize):** This week is structured to build from the cultural/communal (festivals) to the individual/professional (careers), giving students language to talk about both their roots and their future.
### **Week 4 (Course Weeks 15-16): Celebrations, Relationships & Final Projects**
- **W (Where/Why):** This final week is focused on synthesis and performance. The theme is connectionâ€”to people we care about and ideas we have developed. We will focus on polishing the final performance tasks.
- **H (Hook/Hold):** Hook students by sharing heartwarming stories about holiday celebrations or by presenting a challenging relationship dilemma for them to solve.
- **E (Equip/Experience):** Students will read articles about different holidays and relationships. They will listen to podcasts offering relationship advice. We will review **prepositions of time** (in, on, at) for discussing celebrations and **relative clauses** (who, which, that, whose) for adding detail when describing people and things. The majority of class time will be a workshop for drafting essays and recording podcasts.
- **R (Rethink/Reflect):** Students will conduct peer reviews of essay drafts and do practice runs of their podcasts, using a rubric to provide constructive feedback. We will have a final whole-class reflection on the unit's essential questions.
- **E (Evaluate):** This week's formal evaluation consists of the submission of the final **Podcast** and **Persuasive Essay**. The smaller **Holiday Scrapbook** and **Relationship Advice Column** tasks will be the final additions to the portfolio.
- **T (Tailor):** The workshop format allows the instructor to provide tailored, one-on-one feedback to students and groups based on their specific needs for the final projects.
- **O (Organize):** The week is organized as a "project runway," with initial practice, workshop time for development and refinement, and a final showcase/submission of the two major performance tasks.
