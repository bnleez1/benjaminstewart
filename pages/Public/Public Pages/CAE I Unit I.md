# Stage 1 - Desired Results
## Established Goals
- Students will develop and practice their English language skills to improve their communication with others by being active participants within authentic cultural contexts and situations.
- **21st Century Skills**
    - **Communication & Collaboration:** Articulating ideas clearly and effectively; working with diverse teams.
    - **Creativity & Innovation:** Creating original work (presentations, videos, writing).
    - **Information Literacy:** Accessing and evaluating information from various sources (articles, podcasts, videos).
    - **Life & Career Skills:** Demonstrating autonomy and self-direction in learning.
### Transfer
Learners will be able to independently use their learning to‚Ä¶
- Introduce themselves and engage in conversations about personal interests, routines, and experiences in new social or academic settings.
- Plan and describe activities, such as a trip or a recipe, to others in a clear, coherent manner.
- Formulate and express personal opinions on everyday topics like technology, entertainment, and health, supporting their ideas with simple reasoning and examples.
- Navigate and comprehend authentic English-language media (articles, blogs, podcasts, video clips) related to common life themes.
### Meaning
### Understandings:
Learners will understand that...
- Effective communication involves choosing appropriate language, grammar, and vocabulary for different topics, purposes, and situations.
- Language is a tool for expressing personal identity, sharing culture, and connecting with others through stories and experiences.
- Comparing, contrasting, and describing are essential skills for explaining choices, preferences, and observations clearly.
- Improving language skills requires active participation, consistent practice, and being open to constructive feedback.
### Essential Questions:
Learners will keep considering...
- How do we use language to share who we are and what we care about?
- How does our choice of words and grammar change when we talk about the past, present, and future?
- Why is it important to be able to describe and compare things effectively?
- What makes communication successful in different situations (e.g., giving instructions vs. sharing an opinion)?
### Acquisition
### _Learners will know‚Ä¶_
- **Vocabulary:** Personal adjectives, hobby-related terms, time expressions, travel vocabulary, food-related terms (countable/uncountable), technology vocabulary, sports terms, entertainment vocabulary.
- **Grammar:**
    - Present Simple & Continuous
    - Present Perfect
    - Adverbs of Frequency
    - Future Tenses (will/going to)
    - Modals (can, could, may)
    - Comparatives & Superlatives
    - Past Simple & Continuous
### _Students will be skilled at‚Ä¶_
- **Writing:** A self-introduction, descriptions of hobbies and routines, a trip itinerary, a recipe, an opinion essay, a sports report, a movie review.
- **Reading:** Comprehending short biographies, articles, blogs, and reviews on various topics.
- **Listening:** Understanding interviews, conversations, podcasts, news segments, and video clips.
- **Speaking:** Introducing oneself, describing activities, discussing plans and opinions, comparing and contrasting ideas.

## Performance Tasks:
- Students will demonstrate their understanding and transfer of skills by synthesizing topics from the unit to create original content.
    - **Part A: Video Presentation (10 minutes):** Students will create a video presentation on a combination of topics from the unit (e.g., "My Favorite Hobbies and How Technology Has Changed Them" or "A Healthy Daily Routine and the Foods I Cook").     - **Part B: Expository Essay (5 paragraphs):** Students will write a well-structured expository essay on a different combination of topics from the unit. They will be evaluated on organization, coherence, use of supporting details, grammar, vocabulary, and APA formatting.

## Learning Plan Overview
### **Week 1: Personal Identity (Topics: Personal Introductions & Hobbies/Interests)**
- **W:** We will explore how to introduce ourselves and discuss our interests, focusing on using present tenses correctly to describe who we are and what we enjoy.
- **H:** Start with a "Two Truths and a Lie" game where students write three statements about themselves. This immediately gets them talking and using the target language in a fun, low-stakes way.
- **E:**
- **Experience:** Students read short biographies and listen to interviews to identify key phrases for introductions. They practice introducing themselves and a partner.
- **Explore:** In pairs, students discuss hobby-related articles, highlighting new vocabulary. They listen to conversations about hobbies to learn how to ask follow-up questions.
- **Equip:** Direct instruction and practice with Present Simple vs. Continuous and the Present Perfect. Vocabulary building activities for personal adjectives and hobbies.
- **R:** Students write a self-introduction paragraph and share it with a partner for peer feedback (Rethink/Revise) before submitting a final version for the portfolio.
- **E:** Evaluate understanding through the class profile activity, the final self-introduction paragraph, and observation of practice conversations.
- **T:** Provide sentence frames for introductions for students needing support. Challenge advanced students to use more complex adjectives or to compare their hobbies with a partner's.
- **O:** The week is organized to move from receptive skills (reading/listening) to productive skills (speaking/writing), starting with personal identity and moving to personal interests.
### **Week 2: Routines & Plans (Topics: Daily Routines & Travel/Tourism)**
- **W:** This week, we will learn how to describe recurring activities and plan for the future. We'll focus on using adverbs of frequency and future tenses to talk about our daily lives and travel aspirations.
- **H:** Show a short, engaging video clip of a person's "day in the life" (e.g., an athlete, an artist) and have students jot down all the activities they see. Discuss what makes a routine.
- **E:**
- **Experience:** Students read daily routine blogs and listen to a radio show on the topic. They work in groups to create and present an "ideal" daily routine chart.
- **Explore:** Students analyze travel guides and listen to travel podcasts to plan a dream trip itinerary.
- **Equip:** Instruction on Present Simple with adverbs of frequency and future tenses (will vs. going to). Practice creating timelines and itineraries.
- **R:** Students draft a trip itinerary and get peer feedback on its clarity and the correct use of future tenses (Rethink/Revise).
- **E:** Evaluate the daily routine chart and the final travel brochure for clarity, organization, and correct language use.
- **T:** For the travel plan, provide a template for students who need more structure. Encourage advanced students to include contingency plans ("If it rains, we will...").
- **O:** The week progresses from the certainty of daily routines to the possibility of future plans, linking the two themes through the concept of scheduling and planning.
### **Week 3: Describing the World (Topics: Food/Cooking & Technology)**
- **W:** We will focus on descriptive language, learning how to explain a process (like a recipe) and how to express a reasoned opinion (about technology). We'll use nouns, modals, and descriptive adjectives.
- **H:** Bring in (or show pictures of) an unusual food item. Have students work in small groups to describe it and guess what it is.
- **E:**
- **Experience:** Students read various recipes and watch cooking show clips to understand imperative forms and sequential language. They create a short recipe video or presentation.
- **Explore:** Students read articles on tech trends and listen to tech news to gather ideas for a debate on the pros and cons of a specific technology (e.g., social media).
- **Equip:** Direct instruction on countable/uncountable nouns (food) and modals of ability/permission (can, could, may) for discussing technology.
- **R:** Students draft a script for their recipe video and practice it. For the tech debate, students work in teams to build their arguments, anticipating and refuting counter-arguments (Rethink).
- **E:** Evaluate the recipe video/presentation and participation in the tech debate.
- **T:** Provide a vocabulary bank of cooking verbs and tech terms. For the debate, assign specific roles within teams (e.g., opening statement, rebuttal) to ensure all students participate.
- **O:** This week focuses on objects and concepts, moving from a concrete process (cooking) to a more abstract discussion (technology), building descriptive and persuasive language skills.
### **Week 4: Reviews & Recaps (Topics: Sports/Health & Entertainment)**
- **W:** We will wrap up the unit by learning how to recount past events and give reviews. We'll practice using past tenses and comparative/superlative adjectives to share our opinions and experiences. This will be direct preparation for the Midterm Project.
- **H:** Show two different movie trailers for films in the same genre. Have students vote on which they would rather see and explain why, using comparative language.
- **E:**
- **Experience:** Students read sports articles and listen to sports commentary to learn vocabulary for describing events. They write a short report on a real or imaginary game.
- **Explore:** Students read and listen to movie reviews, analyzing the structure and language used to express opinions. They write their own movie review for a class blog.
- **Equip:** Instruction and practice with Past Simple vs. Continuous and comparatives/superlatives.
- **R:** Students exchange their written movie reviews for peer editing, focusing on the use of past tenses and descriptive adjectives (Rethink/Revise). Introduce the Midterm Project, allowing class time for brainstorming and outlining (Rethink).
- **E:** Evaluate the sports report and the final movie review blog post. The final evaluation for the unit is the Midterm Project.
- **T:** Provide templates for the movie review and sports report. Challenge advanced students to use a wider range of past tense structures and more nuanced vocabulary in their reviews and project work.
- **O:** The week is designed to synthesize skills by focusing on recounting and evaluating. It serves as a capstone for the unit's themes and provides a clear runway for students to begin their final performance tasks.
